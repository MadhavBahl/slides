7 Types of Feedback you must ignore

Being Better Series Episode 6

Not every feedback is worth your attention

But how to know when to ignore a feedback?

1. It's coming from a single source

If it comes from a single source, it's an "Opinion"

If it comes from multiple sources, it's a "feedback"

2. It's different from what you often hear

Jimmy's Colleagues: Hey Jimmy, you are doing good work

(That one person who doesn't like Jimmy): Jimmy, your work with "XYZ" project not up to the mark

3. It is "Generic/Vague"

Hey Jimmy, Not sure why, but I feel that your work in the "XYZ" initiative was not up to the mark, it could have been better.

Ask them, how could it have been improved?

4. It's coming from someone who always opposes you

If it is coming from a person who always stands against you (even if you are right), opposes you, and probably hates you or is jealous of you,

It is mostly safe to ignore.

5. It's coming from someone who has a negative attitude

If it is coming from someone who is usually "never happy" and always bad-mouths people, ignore it!

6. It's coming from an unknown source

Feedback from a trusted and credible source is constructive and should be taken seriously

But feedback from an unknown source can be destructive, listen to your instincts

✅ Jimmy's Boss: Hey Jimmy, you could improve your ReactJS skills through this course

❎ (A random guy who has never even coded in JS): Bro, you aren't good enough in JS, let me show you how it is done

✅ Your Gym Trainer: Hey Jimmy, increase your diet a little and with this frequency, you are going good.

❎ (A random guy at gym): You need more protein, buy this supplement

When the feedback is "illogical"

At the end of the day, think before you react!

Most of the "useless" feedbacks are "illogical" as well and easy to spot.
